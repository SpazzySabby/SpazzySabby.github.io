Biography of a Seahorse

Seahorses a.k.a Hippocampus Genus; small but mighty, there are 40 known species found in tropical shallows throughout the world. They are not a staple of diets in the ocean due to the armor like plates covering their body, although crabs love to eat them. Seahorses actually suck at swimming due to the shape of their body, and the fact that they only have one fin: they can die of exhaustion in rough seas if they don't anchor themselves in place with their tails to coral and sea grasses. These tiny wonders eat up to 3,000 brine shrimp in a day, they have no teeth and stomach meaning food goes through them so quickly that they have to eat all the time to stay alive! While there is no concrete evidence about a seahorses natural lifespan, it is estimated they live 1 to 5 years; species dependant.

Specifics About Seahorses

  • They are covered in plates similar to armor, making them hard to digest
  • Seahorses are the chamelions of the sea, changing colors to blend with their environment. This is also helpful in mating, they brighten their colors if they are interested in a mate.
  • Male seahorses are in charge of the eggs, the female gives the male her unfertalized pouch; he fertalizes the eggs and keeps them safe until they are ready to hatch.
  • Each seahorse has its own "fingerprint": a crown on their head called a coral net. The size and design varies on each seahorse making each one unique.
